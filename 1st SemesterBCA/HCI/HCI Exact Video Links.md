# 📝 **Exam-Oriented Short Notes**

Below are **concise bite-sized notes** keyed to typical exam questions.

---

## 📘 Unit I – Introduction & Users

**HCI Definition:** Design of interactive systems _that are usable, useful, and delightful_.

**Human Information Processing:**

- **Sensory → Short-term → Long-term memory**
    
- **Chunking** improves STM capacity (grouping items).
    
- **Fitts’ Law:** Speed of pointing tasks depends on distance & target size.
    

**Models of Interaction:**

- **Norman’s Stages:** goal formation → intention → action → evaluation.
    
- **Gulf of Execution:** user’s difficulty doing action
    
- **Gulf of Evaluation:** difficulty interpreting system state.
    

---

## 📗 Unit II – Design Process

**Design Approach:**

- **Waterfall:** linear phases.
    
- **Iterative:** prototype → feedback → refine.
    

**Universal Design Principles:** _Equitable Use, Flexibility, Simple & Intuitive, Perceptible Information, Tolerance for Error, Low Physical Effort, Size & Space for Approach._

**Design Rules / Heuristics (Jakob Nielsen):**

1. Visibility of system status
    
2. Match between system & real world
    
3. User control and freedom
    
4. Consistency & standards
    
5. Error prevention
    
6. Recognition rather than recall
    
7. Flexibility & efficiency
    
8. Aesthetic & minimalist design
    
9. Help users recognize, diagnose & recover from errors
    
10. Help & documentation. ([Nielsen Norman Group](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design"))
    

---

## 📙 Unit III – Evaluation Techniques

**Expert Review:**

- **Heuristic Evaluation:** experts judge interface against heuristics.
    
- **Cognitive Walkthrough:** step-through tasks to assess learnability.
    

**User Testing:**

- **Think-Aloud Protocol:** users verbalize thoughts during task.
    

**Metrics:** error rates, task time, satisfaction.

---

## 📒 Unit IV – Models & Theories

**Cognitive Models:**

- **GOMS:** Goals, Operators, Methods, Selection rules
    
- **KLM:** estimates task completion time (sum operator times).
    

**Task Analysis:**

- **Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA):** Break task into sub-tasks.
    

---

## 📕 Unit V – Trends & Research

**Ubiquitous Computing:** computing embedded in everyday objects (IoT vision).  
**Information Visualization:** use of charts, graphs, dashboards to reveal patterns.  
**Speech Interfaces:** design where visual feedback is limited.
