#### **I. 20 Short-Answer Questions**

**1. What is the definition of an “Essay” according to the Concise Oxford Dictionary?** An essay is defined as a **literary composition (usually prose and short) on any subject**, serving as a written expression of personal ideas or opinions.

**2. What does the characteristic of “Unity” imply in essay writing?** Unity means an essay must **develop one theme with a definite purpose**, where the subject is clearly defined and maintained throughout, excluding all irrelevant content.

**3. Define “Brevity” as it relates to school essays.** Brevity requires that an essay be **concise and not overly long**, typically around three hundred words, though this varies by subject.

**4. What is the etymological meaning of the word “essay”?** The word “essay” is derived from a verb meaning **“an attempt” or a trial exercise** to express thoughts in good English.

**5. Define the term “Paraphrase.”** Derived from Greek, it means **“equivalent sentence,”** referring to the act of restating a passage's sense using different words while capturing the full meaning.

**6. How does a Précis differ from a Paraphrase in terms of length?** A **précis is much shorter than the original** and involves compression, whereas a paraphrase reproduces both substance and details, often resulting in a text of similar or greater length.

**7. What is “Nominalization”?** Nominalization is a technique that **transforms verb phrases into noun phrases**, often by adding suffixes like ‘-ing’, ‘-tion’, or ‘-ance’ to root words to enhance formality.

**8. Describe the structure of a sentence in the “Active Voice.”** The active voice presents sentences in the **subject-verb-object order**.

**9. What is the primary effect of using the “Passive Voice” in formal writing?** The passive voice reverses the subject-object order to **lend a more impersonal and formal tone** to the writing.

**10. Why should a writer exercise caution when using synonyms?** A writer must ensure synonyms **align with the text’s register** and avoid altering key terms that are crucial to the specific meaning of the text.

**11. What is the purpose of a “Thesis Statement”?** It is a sentence that **encapsulates the essence of the argument** and serves as the core of the essay's structure.

**12. What is a “Topic Sentence”?** A topic sentence is a **succinct opening to a paragraph** that provides a sneak peek into the paragraph’s specific essence and ties it to the thesis.

**13. State the primary goal of “Summarizing.”** The goal is to **capture the main ideas and key points** of a document while maintaining the speaker’s or original author’s ownership of the words.

**14. Define “Reflective Essays.”** Reflective essays consist of **thoughts on abstract topics**, such as habits, social issues, or philosophical and religious subjects.

**15. What distinguishes a “Narrative Essay” from a simple historical account?** A narrative essay should not only narrate events but also **offer reflections and comments** on the incidents or journeys described.

**16. What is the focus of a “Descriptive Essay”?** It involves the **description of places or things**, including animals, plants, buildings, and natural phenomena.

**17. What are “Imaginative Essays”?** These are essays where the writer explores **subjects in imaginative scenarios** they have not experienced in reality, requiring creative thinking.

**18. Define “Expository Essays.”** Expository essays consist of **explanations of subjects**, such as institutions, industries, occupations, or scientific and literary topics.

**19. How should archaisms be handled when paraphrasing poetry into prose?** Archaic or uncommon words should be **substituted with modern equivalents** to ensure the prose is clear and idiomatic.

**20. What is a “Glossary” in a report?** A glossary is a **sequential list of specialized terms** with concise explanations, including abbreviations used within the document.

#### **II. 10 Long-Answer Questions**

**1. Why is effective written communication considered a “bridge” in the modern world, and how does technology influence its prevalence?** **Why:** Written English serves as a vital link that allows individuals from **diverse backgrounds and cultures to understand one another**, especially in a globalized society. **How:** As technology advances, the use of **instant messaging, social platforms, and remote work** has made the written word the primary medium for sharing information and collaborating on projects, making the honing of these skills a necessity for professional success.

**2. Discuss how a writer can achieve “Order” and “Style” in an essay.** **Why:** Without order, an essay is merely a collection of haphazard reflections; without proper style, it fails to engage a professional audience. **How:** To achieve order, a writer must follow a **logical line of thought** that leads to a definite conclusion. Regarding style, a writer should use a **dignified and literary tone**, avoiding slang and colloquialisms while ensuring the writing remains simple, direct, and clear.

**3. Why is a formal tone necessary in essay writing, and how is it maintained?** **Why:** A poised, formal tone **carries significance and credibility**, moving the composition beyond mere subjective viewpoints to an evidence-based academic standard. **How:** It is maintained by **forgoing informality**, using a well-crafted thesis, and supporting all claims with **compelling evidence and concrete details** rather than casual observations.

**4. How does the technique of “Nominalization” enhance the quality of business writing?** **Why:** Nominalization is used to **increase the formality of a text**, making it sound more objective and professional. **How:** This is achieved by **transforming dynamic verb phrases into static noun phrases** (e.g., changing "the profits rose" to "an increase in profits"), which allows the writer to focus on concepts rather than just actions.

**5. Why is paraphrasing considered an excellent exercise in composition, and how does it test a student's ability?** **Why:** It is a valuable method of explanation that **clarifies complex or obscure passages**. **How:** It tests a student’s ability to **concentrate on and understand** what they read; if they cannot restate it simply in their own words, they have not truly grasped the content. It also trains them to express ideas with **grammatical and idiomatic precision**.

**6. Explain the "Method of Procedure" for writing an effective Précis.** **Why:** A précis is required to provide a clear, concise summary of a theme for busy professionals in law, business, or government. **How:** The process involves **three stages**: **Reading** the passage to grasp the general theme and selecting a title; **Writing** a rough draft in one's own words (avoiding direct quotes); and **Revision**, where the draft is compared to the original to ensure all essential points are included while further compressing the text to approximately **one-third of the original length**.

**7. Why is summarizing often overlooked in daily communication, and how can it resolve conflicts?** **Why:** People often **assume they already know what others are feeling**, leading to ineffective communication and misunderstandings. **How:** Summarizing resolves conflicts by acting as a **co-operative cycle** where the listener repeats the speaker's points. This allows the speaker to **"hear themselves,"** gain insights into their own emotions, and confirm that they have been accurately understood.

**8. How should the "Main Body" of an essay be structured to maintain coherence?** **Why:** Coherence ensures that the reader can follow the argument without confusion. **How:** The main body should consist of paragraphs where **each tackles a single pivotal concept** bound to the thesis. Every paragraph must begin with a **topic sentence**, bolster claims with **illustrative examples**, and revisit the **purpose verb** originally introduced in the thesis.

**9. Why is it important to vary sentence types in written communication, and how does it impact the reader?** **Why:** Using only one type of sentence can make writing monotonous and difficult to process. **How:** By mixing **simple, compound, and complex sentences**, a writer can make the text **more engaging and easier to read**. This variety helps emphasize different points and prevents the "run-on" or "fragment" errors that occur when writers lack control over sentence structure.

**10. How does a writer effectively paraphrase poetry while maintaining the "essence" of the work?** **Why:** Poetry often uses unusual word orders and archaic language that can obscure the meaning in a prose context. **How:** The writer must **substitute archaic words with modern equivalents** and **simplify flowery language**. They must also **avoid rhythm and rhyme** in the prose version, focusing instead on grasping the **central idea** rather than translating the poem line-by-line.

#### **III. 5 Writing/Drafting Tasks**

**Task 1: Nominalization and Voice Correction** **Prompt:** Rewrite the following paragraph using **nominalization** to make it more formal, and change the final sentence to **passive voice**: "We interviewed the customers to see why they are buying more. We found that they like the new design. This department will analyze the results."

**Model Answer:** **Interviews were conducted** with customers to determine the reasons for the **increase in purchasing**. The findings indicate a **preference for the new design**. **The results will be analyzed by this department**.

**Task 2: Précis Writing** **Prompt:** Write a **Précis** of the following passage (approx. 1/3 length): "Machines were made to be man’s servant; yet he has grown so dependent on them that they are in a way becoming his masters. Already most men spend most of their lives looking after and waiting upon machines. They must be fed with coal, and given petrol to drink and oil to wash with and must be kept at the right temperature."

**Model Answer:** **The Mastership of Machines** Though designed as servants, machines are becoming man's masters due to heavy dependency. Humans now spend significant time maintaining these devices, which demand constant fueling and specialized care to remain functional.

**Task 3: Drafting a Memorandum** **Prompt:** Draft a **Memo** from the Head of IT to all staff regarding a mandatory update for the company's cybersecurity protocol.

**Model Answer:** **Memorandum** **To:** All Staff Members **From:** [Your Name], Head of IT **Date:** October 24, 2023 **Subject:** Mandatory Cybersecurity Protocol Update Please be advised that the company is updating its cybersecurity protocols to enhance data protection. **A mandatory training session** on these new procedures is scheduled for Friday at 10:00 AM in the main conference hall. Attendance is required to ensure a smooth transition and maintain system security. [Your Signature].

**Task 4: Paraphrasing Poetry** **Prompt:** Paraphrase the following lines into a clear prose sentence: "Beside a hill, let my dwelling be, a humble cot."

**Model Answer:** **I wish to have a small, simple cottage located next to a hill**.

**Task 5: Essay Introduction/Thesis Construction** **Prompt:** Using the "Elephant" essay example, draft an **Introduction** including a **Thesis Statement** based on its physical characteristics and utility.

**Model Answer:** **The Elephant: A Majestic Servant** The elephant is one of the most remarkable creatures in the animal kingdom, distinguished by its massive size and unique features. While it is often admired for its strength in the wild, its true value is seen in its historical and modern interactions with humans. **This essay will argue that the elephant’s unique physical adaptations and intelligence make it an indispensable asset to human industry and culture**.
