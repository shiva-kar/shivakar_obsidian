**I. 20 Short-Answer Questions**

**1. What is the etymological origin of the word "communication"?** The term originates from the Latin word **“communis,” which means "common"**.

**2. How does Lelan Brown define communication?** Lelan Brown defines it as the **interchange of facts, ideas, feelings, or courses of action**.

**3. What are the five stages of the communication process?** The stages are **formulation of the message, encoding, transmission, decoding and interpretation, and feedback and evaluation**.

**4. What is "Feedback" in the context of communication?** Feedback is the **recipient’s acknowledgement of the sender’s message**, indicating the level of understanding or agreement.

**5. Define "Vertical Communication."** Vertical communication follows well-defined lines where **policy decisions move downward** from top management and **feedback or performance data moves upward** from subordinates.

**6. What is "Horizontal Communication"?** It is the exchange of information between **departments or individuals at the same level of authority** to facilitate coordination.

**7. List the four types of noise that can disrupt communication.** The four types are **physiological, physical, psychological, and semantic noise**.

**8. Provide three examples of psychological barriers to communication.** Examples include **distrust of the communicator, premature evaluation, and limited perception**.

**9. What are semantic barriers?** Semantic barriers involve **problems with language usage**, such as jargon, poorly chosen words, or awkward sentence structures.

**10. Define "Proxemics."** Proxemics is the **study of the nature, degree, and impact of the spatial distance** people naturally maintain in various social and interpersonal situations.

**11. What is "Kinesics"?** Kinesics is the **study of body movement**, including posture and gestures, and how they convey meaning.

**12. Define "Paralinguistics."** Paralinguistics refers to **vocal characteristics beyond words**, such as tone, pitch, volume, and modulation.

**13. What is "Haptics"?** Haptics refers to the **use of touch** as a method of non-verbal communication.

**14. What are the four zones of proxemic behavior identified by Edward T. Hall?** The zones are **close space (50 cm), personal space (1 m), social space (1-2 m), and public space (more than 2 m)**.

**15. Define "Active Listening."** Active listening is a mode that **demonstrates interest and understanding of the speaker** by engaging and confirming comprehension.

**16. What is "Discriminative Listening"?** It is listening specifically for **certain details while ignoring others**, such as a parent hearing a child crying.

**17. Define "Empathetic Listening."** Empathetic listening involves **understanding the speaker’s view by putting oneself in their position** to show empathy.

**18. What is the difference between speech rate and thought rate?** People generally speak at **125 to 175 words per minute**, while the mind can process **400 to 800 words per minute**.

**19. What are "Back-channel prompts"?** These are verbal and non-verbal signals like **“uh-huh” or nodding** that show a listener is being attentive.

**20. What is "Diagonal Communication"?** It involves communication between individuals who are **not in the same department or hierarchy**, used for cross-functional exchange.

**II. 10 Long-Answer Questions**

**1. Why is the "Audience" considered the most important element in communication planning, and how should a communicator prioritize them?** **Why:** Aristotle and modern theorists emphasize that the audience determines the **oratory style, content, and medium** required for the message to be accepted. **How:** A communicator must prioritize the audience by determining **what, how, when, where, and who** delivers the message based on the audience's characteristics and susceptibility.

**2. Discuss the significance of the "Feedback" loop in ensuring effective two-way communication.** **Why:** Without feedback, a sender cannot know if the message was received or interpreted correctly, making communication a one-way street prone to error. **How:** Effective communication **encourages feedback** through gestures, facial expressions, or seeking clarification, allowing for **adjustments in content and presentation** until the meaning is clear to both parties.

**3. Explain how "Proxemics" influences interpersonal relationships in a business setting.** **Why:** Spatial distance communicates the **level of intimacy and trust** between individuals. **How:** Professionals maintain different "zones": **Close space** is for intimate relations, while **social space** (1-2 meters) is used for strangers or new acquaintances. Understanding these zones prevents **negative emotions** caused by uninvited intrusions into personal territory.

**4. Describe the five stages of the Listening Process and their role in comprehension.** **Why:** Listening is a continuous, interactive procedure rather than a linear sequence. **How:** It involves **Receiving** (sensory input), **Interpreting** (making sense of cues), **Recalling** (memory storage), **Evaluating** (judging credibility), and **Responding** (demonstrating attentiveness).

**5. How do "Semantic Barriers" hinder organizational effectiveness, and how can they be minimized?** **Why:** Language does not have inherent meaning; it has the **potential for meaning**, which can be lost if words are poorly chosen. **How:** They are minimized by avoiding **jargon, platitudes, and awkward sentence structures**, and by choosing **clear, concise language** that aligns with the recipient's vocabulary.

**6. Discuss the various functions of Non-verbal Communication.** **Why:** Non-verbal cues are often **more instinctive and automatic** than verbal ones, making them more credible. **How:** They function by **reinforcing** verbal messages (pointing while speaking), **substituting** for words (hand signals in loud places), or **regulating** conversational flow (pitch changes to signal turn-taking).

**7. Why is "Active Listening" crucial in professional settings, and what strategies support it?** **Why:** It creates a **positive communication environment** and fosters participation and motivation among employees. **How:** Strategies include **facing the speaker, maintaining eye contact, minimizing distractions**, and asking **clarifying questions** to ensure accurate understanding.

**8. Explain the impact of the "Speech-Thought Rate Gap" on listening and how to use it constructively.** **Why:** The brain processes information **much faster than a person speaks**, leading to "side-processing" and potential distractions. **How:** A constructive listener uses this extra cognitive capacity to **reinforce the essential message**, mentally summarizing points rather than succumbing to daydreaming or narcissism.

**9. Compare and contrast "Formal" and "Informal" communication channels in an organization.** **Why:** Both are necessary for a functioning workforce, but they serve different purposes. **How:** **Formal channels** follow the established chain of command (line, functional, and staff relationships), while **informal channels (the grapevine)** arise spontaneously when employees feel uninformed by the formal system.

**10. How do "Physical Barriers" disrupt communication, and what environmental factors are involved?** **Why:** External physical conditions can overwhelm the cognitive ability to process a message. **How:** This includes **noise** from the environment, **high temperatures or poor ventilation**, and **physical distance** that inhibits immediate interaction.

**Analogy for Understanding:** Think of the **communication process** as a **radio broadcast**: the **Sender** is the station, the **Encoding** is the signal frequency, the **Channel** is the airwaves, the **Decoding** is the listener’s radio tuning in, and the **Static** is the **Noise**. Without **Feedback** (the listener calling the station), the broadcaster never knows if the music is actually being heard or if they are just playing to an empty room.
