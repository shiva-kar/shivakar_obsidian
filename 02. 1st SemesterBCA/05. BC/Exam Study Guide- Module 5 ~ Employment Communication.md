**I. 20 Short-Answer Questions**

**1. What is the primary definition and purpose of a report?** A report is a **written document tailored for a specific audience** that aims to present, analyze, and apply information and evidence to address a particular issue.

**2. State the two main purposes for assigning reports in an educational or professional setting.** Reports are used to **assess understanding of subject matter** and to provide practical experience in a valuable skill used in professional environments.

**3. Define the "Synopsis" or "Abstract" of a report.** The synopsis is a **brief description of the report's content**, typically covering objectives, findings, and the timeframe in about half a page.

**4. What information is contained in the "Terms of Reference"?** This section provides a brief explanation of **who will read the report**, the reason for writing it, and the methods used to gather information.

**5. What is the purpose of the "Appendices" section in a report?** The appendices include **supporting data that is not published** in the main body, such as tables, graphs, surveys, or records.

**6. Define a "Glossary of Technical Terms" as used in report writing.** It is a **sequential list of specialized terms** with concise explanations, including abbreviations or standard units used throughout the document.

**7. What is a Resume?** A resume is a **comprehensive document or summary** of an individual’s education, professional background, qualifications, and skills used for job applications.

**8. What characterizes a "Chronological Resume"?** A chronological resume is best for jobseekers with a **consistent development in one division** throughout their career.

**9. Define a "Functional Resume" and state who it is ideal for.** A functional resume focuses on skills and is ideal for jobseekers who have been **self-employed or have gaps** in their employment history.

**10. What is a "Combined Resume"?** A combined resume is perfect for jobseekers with a **specific range of abilities** or those looking to change career paths.

**11. What does the acronym "ATS" refer to in the resume screening process?** ATS stands for **Applicant Tracking System**, which some companies use to evaluate resumes and allow only those that meet specific requirements to be reviewed by a manager.

**12. What is the generally accepted length for a standard resume?** The most widely accepted length for a resume is **one A4-page**, accompanied by a one-page introductory cover letter.

**13. Define "Group Discussion" (GD).** A Group Discussion is a **structured process where a collection of individuals** engage in face-to-face interaction to exchange ideas or achieve a common goal.

**14. How many participants are typically involved in a standard GD?** A Group Discussion usually consists of **7-12 participants**.

**15. List three specific character traits assessed during a Group Discussion.** GDs assess **communication skills, leadership abilities, and problem-solving skills**.

**16. What is the "Keyword Approach" in a Group Discussion?** The Keyword Approach involves **defining the dominant words in the topic** to develop a logical structure for the conversation.

**17. What are the "Three Cs" used to rank leadership in a GD?** The three "Cs" are **Clarity, Content, and Certainty**.

**18. Define "Horizontal Thinking" in the context of GD.** Horizontal thinking is the ability to **think on your feet** and contribute with creative and unconventional insights, especially for abstract topics.

**19. What are "Analytical Skills" as assessed in a GD?** Analytical skills show a candidate's ability to **effectively flowchart their perspective** and investigate a theme in a comprehensive, logical manner.

**20. What is a "Mock GD Session"?** A Mock GD is a **practice session** where participants simulate a real group discussion to receive feedback on their performance and leadership skills.

**II. 10 Long-Answer Questions**

**1. Explain the importance of the "Discussion" section in a report and how it should be structured.** **Why:** The Discussion is the **main body of the report** where the writer analyzes facts and evidence related to the central problem. **How:** It should be divided into **subsections with clear headings** for clarity, utilizing bullet points for lists and ensuring all sources are properly cited. This section is where the writer relates gathered evidence to the specific issue stated in the brief.

**2. Discuss the seven stages of the report-writing process as outlined in the sources.** **Why:** Careful planning ensures the report is **clear, concise, and well-structured**. **How:** The process includes **understanding the brief**, gathering data, organizing material, **analyzing evidence**, writing the first draft, reviewing/redrafting, and finally, focusing on the **presentation and proofreading**.

**3. Why is "readability" considered the most important factor in a good resume?** **Why:** Hiring managers spend a **minimal amount of time** evaluating each resume they receive. **How:** A resume must have a **well-organized layout** that is free of errors and presents information concisely. Poor readability may cause a resume to be **discarded without consideration**, so clear fonts and strategic formatting like bolding or italics are essential.

**4. How can a jobseeker effectively "tailor" their resume for a specific employer?** **Why:** Tailoring ensures the applicant stands out as the **perfect fit for the specific job opportunity**. **How:** The jobseeker should use the **job description to highlight matching skills** and research the organization to understand the qualities hiring managers seek. This includes using **strategically chosen keywords** that might be recognized by an Applicant Tracking System.

**5. Discuss why Group Discussions (GDs) have become a popular method for assessing candidates.** **Why:** While written tests evaluate IQ and knowledge, GDs assess **emotional intelligence and social skills**. **How:** The process reveals a candidate's **attitude toward others**, their listening capacity, and their ability to perform under pressure in a group setting. It serves as a **situation test** to see if a candidate possesses the interpersonal abilities necessary to achieve common organizational goals.

**6. Explain the significance of "Group Dynamics" in a Group Discussion.** **Why:** Success in business relies on the **ability to find a balance** between individual excellence and group performance. **How:** A candidate scoring high in group dynamics demonstrates **team skills and a willingness to acknowledge** diverse perspectives. This trait indicates to employers that the individual will likely **contribute effectively to the organization** rather than working in isolation.

**7. How should a participant handle a GD topic with which they are unfamiliar?** **Why:** It is a common mistake to initiate a discussion without **sufficient knowledge**, which can lead to an embarrassing silence. **How:** If the topic is unfamiliar, the participant should **listen to others attentively** first to gather ideas. Once they have grasped the context, they should make **short, relevant contributions** and support their points with whatever facts they can logically deduce.

**8. Describe the role of a leader in facilitating an "Open Process" during a Group Discussion.** **Why:** A productive environment requires that **no single individual dominates** the conversation. **How:** The leader must ensure that **everyone has an opportunity** to express their thoughts and should be particularly attentive to quieter participants. They must also safeguard **"minority rights,"** allowing unconventional ideas to be discussed respectfully rather than dismissed.

**9. Why is it detrimental to "monopolize" or "argue loudly" during a Group Discussion?** **Why:** Group discussions are meant to be **collaborative, not combative**. **How:** Dominating the conversation or shouting reflects a **lack of social behavior and leadership potential**. Such actions disrupt the **consensus-building process** and can cause a candidate to be rated negatively by moderators for a lack of humility and tolerance.

**10. How do "Additional Sections" like Volunteer Work and Personal Interests add value to a resume?** **Why:** Resumes should go beyond academic and professional accomplishments to **form a mental image** of the candidate. **How:** Subsections for **internships, languages, or volunteer work** demonstrate creativity and specific personality traits. These details help employers **gain insights into a candidate's character** and how they might fit into the company culture.

**III. 5 Writing/Drafting Tasks**

**Task 1: Drafting a Report Synopsis** **Prompt:** Draft a **Synopsis (Abstract)** for a report investigating the implementation of solar energy in an office complex.

**Model Answer:** **Synopsis** This report examines the **feasibility and cost-effectiveness** of transitioning the [Company Name] office complex to solar energy. The investigation was conducted over a **three-month period** and involved energy audits and vendor evaluations. **Findings indicate** that while initial installation costs are high, the projected **long-term savings and carbon footprint reduction** align with corporate sustainability goals. The report concludes with a **recommendation for a phased rollout** beginning in the next fiscal year.

**Task 2: Resume Objective Writing** **Prompt:** Write a **Resume Objective** for a recent graduate seeking an entry-level position in Business Administration.

**Model Answer:** **Objective** **Diligent Business Administration graduate** seeking an entry-level role at [Company Name] to utilize strong **analytical and interpersonal skills** developed through academic research and internship experience. Committed to **contributing to organizational efficiency** while further developing professional expertise in project management and team collaboration.

**Task 3: Group Discussion Leadership Scenario** **Prompt:** You are in a GD where one participant is being ignored. Write a **facilitator statement** to bring them into the conversation.

**Model Answer:** "I think we have some **intriguing points here regarding leadership and knowledge sharing**. However, I noticed that Daisy has been trying to contribute; **Daisy, do you have something you would like to add** to this specific idea so we can consider your perspective before moving forward?".

**Task 4: Correcting Resume "Don'ts"** **Prompt:** The following resume content contains several errors. Correct it based on the sources: "I am the best at what I do. I have ten hobbies including cat grooming. I used a fancy cursive font."

**Model Answer:**

1. **Avoid generic statements or cliché phrases** such as "I am the best at what I do"; use specific, tailored statements instead.

2. **Do not include irrelevant information** or experiences; focus only on those significant to the job.

3. **Opt for easily readable and professional fonts** rather than casual or cursive styles that hinder readability.
